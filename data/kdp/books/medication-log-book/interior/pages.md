## Page 2: How to Use This Book

```
How to Use This Log

Use the Medication Profile pages at the front to record the
full details of each medication you take -- name, prescriber,
pharmacy, dose, and instructions. Then use the Daily Log pages
to check off each dose as you take it. If you miss a dose, write
"MISS" in the box rather than leaving it blank so you can track
patterns. Use the Refill Tracker to note when refills are due
and when they were picked up. Bring this book to every medical
appointment and to the pharmacy -- it is your master medication
record and may be critical in an emergency.
```

## Page 3: Quick Reference Card

```
MEDICATION SAFETY QUICK REFERENCE

MISSED DOSE GENERAL RULES
• If you miss a dose and it's close to your next scheduled dose,
  skip the missed dose and resume your regular schedule.
• NEVER double-dose unless specifically told to by your prescriber.
• For critical medications (blood thinners, seizure meds,
  insulin) -- call your doctor or pharmacist immediately if unsure.
• Note "MISS" in your daily log so you can report it accurately.

COMMON ABBREVIATIONS
  QD   = Once daily
  BID  = Twice daily
  TID  = Three times daily
  QID  = Four times daily
  PRN  = As needed (not scheduled)
  AC   = Before meals
  PC   = After meals
  HS   = At bedtime
  SL   = Under the tongue (sublingual)
  PO   = By mouth (oral)
  Mg   = Milligrams
  mcg  = Micrograms
  mL   = Milliliters

STORAGE REMINDERS
  □ Store at room temperature (59–77°F / 15–25°C) unless labeled otherwise
  □ Keep away from moisture -- do NOT store in bathrooms
  □ Some medications require refrigeration (36–46°F / 2–8°C)
  □ Keep all medications out of reach of children and pets
  □ Do not use medications past their expiration date

IN AN EMERGENCY
  Show this book to any EMT, ER nurse, or emergency provider.
  Your complete medication list begins on page 4.
```
