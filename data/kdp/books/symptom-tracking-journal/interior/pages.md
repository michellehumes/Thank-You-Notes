## Page 2 — How to Use This Book

```
HOW TO USE THIS JOURNAL

Fill in one spread per day. Log your symptoms as they occur or at the
end of each day. Bring this journal to every medical appointment — your
doctor needs patterns, not memories. Use the Pain Scale reference on
the next page to keep your ratings consistent across entries.

Consistency matters more than completeness. Even a partial entry is
better than a blank one. Over time, your log will reveal triggers,
cycles, and responses to treatments that no single appointment can
capture.

[tip box, light border]
TIP: Keep this journal on your nightstand. Rate your sleep quality
first thing each morning while it's fresh.
```

## Page 3 — Quick Reference Card: Pain & Symptom Scale

```
QUICK REFERENCE: SYMPTOM SEVERITY SCALE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAIN SCALE
  0  │ No pain
  1  │ Very mild, barely noticeable
  2  │ Minor, annoying but ignorable
  3  │ Noticeable, distracting at times
  4  │ Moderate, can work through it
  5  │ Moderately strong, hard to ignore
  6  │ Moderately strong, affects concentration
  7  │ Severe, limits daily activities
  8  │ Intense, very hard to function
  9  │ Excruciating, unable to function
 10  │ Worst imaginable, requires immediate care

ENERGY SCALE
  1–3  │ Bedridden / extremely fatigued
  4–6  │ Limited activity, resting often
  7–9  │ Functional with moderate energy
   10  │ Full energy, no limitations

MOOD SCALE
  1–3  │ Very low, depressed, hopeless
  4–6  │ Neutral to mildly positive
  7–9  │ Good, positive, engaged
   10  │ Excellent, joyful

SLEEP QUALITY
  Poor │ Woke many times, unrefreshed
  Fair │ Some interruptions, groggy
  Good │ Minor issues, mostly rested
  Best │ Slept through, fully refreshed
```
