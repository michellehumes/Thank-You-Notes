## Printing Tips for Best Results

- **Paper:** Print on **white or cream cardstock (65–80 lb)** for a sturdy, premium card feel. Standard printer paper works too, but cardstock really elevates it.
- **Home printer:** Use the **"actual size"** or **"fit to page"** setting for correct dimensions. Both inkjet and laser printers work great.
- **Print shop:** Save as a PDF and bring it to FedEx Office, Staples, Walgreens Photo, or your local copy shop for even crisper color output.
- **Folding:** Print on one side, fold in half, and you have a ready-to-sign greeting card.
